# Brand Guidelines

## Brand Overview

This site focuses on AI in marketing, coding experiments, and thought leadership in content strategy.

## Brand Values

- **Innovation**: We explore cutting-edge approaches to content and marketing
- **Transparency**: We share both successes and failures honestly
- **Practicality**: We focus on actionable insights over theory
- **Experimentation**: We're not afraid to try new things and learn publicly

## Content Themes

### Primary Topics
1. AI in marketing organizations
2. Content strategy and operations
3. Coding and automation experiments
4. GitHub Copilot and AI-assisted development

### Secondary Topics
- Marketing technology
- Team collaboration
- Workflow automation
- Developer tools and practices

## Visual Style

### Colors
- Primary: Professional blue tones
- Accent: Subtle highlights for emphasis
- Background: Clean whites with dark mode support
- Text: High contrast for readability

### Typography
- Headers: Bold and clear
- Body: Readable sans-serif font
- Code: Monospace font for technical content
- Emphasis: Use bold sparingly, italics for subtle emphasis

## Content Personality

### We Are
- Thoughtful and analytical
- Experimental and curious
- Honest about challenges
- Focused on practical outcomes

### We Are Not
- Overly promotional or salesy
- Theoretical without application
- Dismissive of traditional approaches
- Afraid to admit mistakes

## Key Messages

1. **AI is a tool**: Not a replacement for human creativity and strategy
2. **Experimentation matters**: Public learning benefits everyone
3. **Content strategy is collaboration**: Not top-down control
4. **Automation enables creativity**: By removing repetitive work

## Writing for This Brand

### Do
- Share personal experiences and learnings
- Provide concrete examples and code samples
- Acknowledge trade-offs and limitations
- Link to relevant resources and references

### Don't
- Make absolute claims without evidence
- Dismiss reader concerns or questions
- Oversimplify complex topics
- Copy content without attribution

## Audience

### Primary Audience
- Marketing leaders and practitioners
- Content strategists and operations professionals
- Developers interested in AI tools
- Teams exploring automation

### Audience Needs
- Practical guidance on implementing AI
- Real-world examples and case studies
- Technical how-tos and tutorials
- Strategic frameworks and thinking

## Competitive Differentiation

Unlike other marketing/AI content:
- We show the work (code, experiments, results)
- We focus on organizations, not just individuals
- We bridge technical and strategic perspectives
- We share failures as well as successes

## Content Quality Standards

- All articles must provide actionable value
- Technical content must include working examples
- Claims must be supported with evidence or clearly marked as opinion
- Code samples must be tested and functional
- Links must be verified and relevant

## Collaboration Principles

When working with contributors:
- Respect different perspectives and experiences
- Provide constructive feedback
- Focus on improving the content, not the person
- Celebrate good work and give credit
- Default to transparency in communication
