# Content Style Guide

This document serves as a reference for all content creation within this repository.

## Voice and Tone

- **Voice**: Professional yet approachable, authoritative without being condescending
- **Tone**: Conversational, clear, and engaging
- **Perspective**: Second person ("you") when addressing readers, first person ("I", "we") when sharing experiences

## Writing Principles

1. **Clarity First**: Write clearly and concisely. Avoid jargon unless it's standard in the field
2. **Active Voice**: Prefer active voice over passive voice
3. **Short Sentences**: Keep sentences concise and to the point
4. **Scannable**: Use headers, bullet points, and short paragraphs to make content easy to scan

## Formatting Standards

### Headers
- Use title case for H1 (main title)
- Use sentence case for H2 and H3
- Keep headers descriptive and actionable

### Lists
- Use bullet points for unordered lists
- Use numbered lists only when order matters
- Keep list items parallel in structure

### Links
- Use descriptive anchor text (avoid "click here")
- Link to authoritative sources
- Open external links in new tabs when appropriate

## Content Structure

### Introduction
- Hook the reader in the first paragraph
- Clearly state what they'll learn or gain
- Keep it to 2-3 sentences

### Body
- Break content into logical sections
- Use subheadings to guide the reader
- Include examples and practical applications
- Support claims with data or references

### Conclusion
- Summarize key takeaways
- Include a call to action when appropriate
- Leave the reader with something to think about

## SEO Guidelines

- **Title**: 50-60 characters, include primary keyword
- **Meta Description**: 150-160 characters, compelling and descriptive
- **Keywords**: 3-5 relevant keywords, naturally integrated
- **Headers**: Include keywords in H2s naturally
- **Content Length**: Aim for 800-2000 words for substantive articles

## Brand Terms and Spelling

- AI (all caps, not "ai" or "A.I.")
- GitHub (camel case)
- JavaScript (camel case)
- Use American English spelling
- Company names as they officially appear

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Don't use excessive exclamation points
- Avoid clichÃ©s and buzzwords
- Don't make unsupported claims
- Avoid using "very" and other weak modifiers
- Don't write in all caps for emphasis

## Examples

### Good Example
> AI is transforming how marketing teams work. By automating repetitive tasks, teams can focus on strategy and creativity. Here's how to get started.

### Poor Example  
> AI IS REVOLUTIONIZING EVERYTHING!!! Marketing is being completely disrupted by artificial intelligence and it's very exciting. You should really check it out.
